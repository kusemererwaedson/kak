Investor FAQ

How is this program different from venture fund investing?

Rubiz Rolling Fund is structured as a series of quarterly funds.

Unlike investing in a venture fund, when a client invests in Rubiz Rolling Fund:

1. A Client follows a flexible, quarterly investment schedule rather than a one-time commitment to a fund. A Client participates in investments Rubiz Rolling Fund makes for each quarterly fund the Client invests in.

2. Clients can subscribe for future quarterly funds in advance and easily commit more or less capital as their investment goals change.

How do subscriptions work?

New quarterly funds in this Program are created each quarter to invest in on-thesis deals sourced during the quarter. By subscribing, a Client commits to make the subscribed-for capital contribution to each quarterly fund offered through this Program. Each Client is then able to modify or cancel its subscription before the beginning of any subsequent quarterly fund, after meeting the number of quarterly subscriptions the Client initially commits to.

A Client's subscription into a quarterly fund also provides that investable capital remaining at the end of the quarterly fund's deployment period may be automatically rolled over into a subsequent quarterly fund. When this happens, a Client receives a corresponding contribution to the subsequent quarterly fund in addition to whatever amount it subscribes for and contributes directly into the subsequent quarterly fund.

How do management and admin fees work?

At this point, the funds offered through the Program will not pay a management fee. Rubiz Rolling Fund may choose to increase the management fee of funds offered through this Program, at which point all existing subscribers will have the opportunity to reconfirm their subscription and such increase.

TIG Management Centre (TIGMC) launches and maintains each quarterly fund throughout its lifetime. Generally, TIGMC charges a 1.5% admin fee for the maintenance of each quarterly fund’s 5-year lifetime, subject to a Ugx100,000 minimum. Ugx100,000 is paid up front to TIGMC for the setup of each quarterly fund, and the remaining fee is paid out quarterly over the course of the fund’s lifetime.

How does carried interest work?

Carried interest payment is calculated across a Client's subscription period. The subscription period is the number of quarters to which a Client subscribes in advance. Depending on the options provided by the fund manager, a Client may have a quarter-by-quarter subscription period or a multi-quarter subscription period.

If on a quarter-by-quarter subscription period, the Client must be repaid only the amount they contributed to the quarterly fund experiencing the exit before that Client begins to pay carried interest.

Certain Clients may pay more or less carry at the discretion of Rubiz Rolling Fund or the funds' investment adviser.

For Rubiz Rolling Fund, a Client will pay 30% total carried interest once they have received back 100% of their contributions made during each subscription term.

How do distributions work?

Subscribing Clients participate only in distributions from the quarterly funds to which they contribute. A Client has no interest in portfolio investments of quarterly funds formed before their subscription was accepted or after it is canceled or rejected.

Annual distributions – interim and final: …

As a quarterly fund exits its investments, Clients in that quarterly fund will receive distributions net of fees and any applicable carry. Funds that have not exited all investments by the end of their lifespan will ordinarily be liquidated, subject to the terms of the applicable fund documents. Clients should be prepared to hold their interest in each quarterly fund for its full term.

Will a Client participate in all investments made by the funds offered through this Program?

A Client will only participate in investments made by the quarterly funds they subscribed to. If a Client cancels or fails to fund their subscription, the Client will no longer participate in future quarterly funds (subject to any reinvestment of rollover capital). A failure to fund a subscription may be subject to customary fund penalties.

Will a Client's percentage ownership of quarterly funds always be the same?

No. A Client's pro rata share of funds under this Program may change from quarter-to-quarter based on changes in its subscription amount and the subscriptions made by other Clients. For example, if new Clients join this Program over time and an existing Client does not increase its subscription amount, its pro rata share will decrease in future funds.

Are Clients guaranteed access to Rubiz Rolling Fund's future funds?

No. TIGPE Rolling Fund or TIGPE may cancel a Client's subscription in upcoming quarterly funds or cease offering funds under this Program altogether at any time. Also, investors in this Program will have no right to participate in funds offered outside of this Program.

How are subscriptions funded?

Subscriptions are funded either through wire or via ACH transfer using information submitted through the TIGPE Investments Platform. Clients must fund their subscriptions by the funding deadline indicated on the Platform. If a Client authorizes payment of its subscription by ACH and the Client's TIGPE Investment Platform account balance doesn't cover the Client's full subscription, the remaining balance will be transferred automatically via ACH from the Client's bank account on the funding deadline date.

Clients will generally transfer their subscription amount once per quarter. If the initial close of the Program takes place after a fiscal quarter has already begun, the period between the first and second transfers may be less than a quarter.

How does changing or canceling a subscription work?

Typically, Clients may increase, decrease, or cancel their subscription amount at any time prior to the subscription deadline for a quarterly fund. Clients that subscribe with a subscription period of two or more quarters must fulfill their subscription period commitments before decreasing or canceling their subscription. Clients can make updates to their subscription from their portfolio and from the Rolling Fund's fund page.

Unless modified, a Client's subscription amount and period will auto-renew such that after a subscription period ends, another subscription period of the same quarterly length and commitment amount begins.

Who manages these funds?

Investment opportunities are sourced by TIGPE and reviewed by TIGMC, the funds’ investment adviser. TIGMC generally follows TIGPE's investment recommendation in allocating the funds' capital but may reject on-thesis investment opportunities in its sole discretion when TIGPE discloses a conflict of interest or due to other extenuating circumstances.

Do funds in this Program have minimum or maximum investment sizes?

The funds’ minimum investment into a portfolio partnership/ company/ lease will be Ugx10million. The funds in this Program will not have maximum investment sizes into portfolio companies.

What happens if funds in this Program do not invest their total capital each quarter?

A fund will deploy capital into investments until the end of its investment period, which usually occurs at the end of a quarter.

If any quarterly fund in the Program does not invest all of its investable capital during its investment period, Clients' proportionate share of capital that is not held back for fund expenses may be automatically reinvested into the next quarterly fund. Such rollover amounts are treated as additional capital contribution on top of a Client's existing quarterly subscription amount. Clients are not guaranteed rollover contributions and TIGPE may elect to distribute such amounts directly to Clients at the end of a quarterly fund's investment period. Any rejected amount will be returned to a subscriber's TIGPE investor account.

When will Clients learn about partnerships/ companies/ leases the funds invested in?

There is no guarantee that Clients in the Program will receive information about the funds and underlying portfolio companies. Fund updates, if any, will be provided solely at TIGPE's discretion.

Can Clients opt out of specific deals?

No. Clients are subscribing to invest in a series of consecutively offered quarterly funds, in exchange for access to the funds' investments. Clients cannot choose whether to invest in specific deals.

Can Clients sell their investment in the fund?

Clients generally cannot sell or transfer their interest outside of serious events like death or divorce.

Can Clients redeem their interests in the fund?

No, Clients generally do not have the right to redeem their interests.

When will funds launched under this Program begin deploying capital?

The initial quarterly fund in this Program may warehouse investments or otherwise deploy all or a portion of its capital prior to accepting and closing investor subscriptions.

How do I receive tax documents from this Program?

Each Client will receive a WHT Credit Certificate for each quarterly fund to which they are subscribed. To streamline reporting, TIGMC will aggregate all annual WHT Credit Certificates into a single package, which will be sent to each Client.
