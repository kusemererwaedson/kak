About Us

We promote bamboo-based agroforestry by empowering farmers through propagation and supply of quality planting materials, providing plantation best practices, processing and marketing, and facilitating sustainable and impactful capital flow

Our Mission

To promote bamboo-based agroforestry through supplying quality planting materials, plantation development best practices, processing and marketing, and effective and impactful circulation of capital

Our Vision

To lead in the conception and implementation of agricultural projects in Uganda, with a focus on designing and building agricultural farms and related value chain linkages.

Why Farmers Choose Us

1. Superior Quality
Planting Materials

Through Tissue Culture Micropropagation, we produce required quantities (availability), and varied species. Farmers trust us for regular, reliable supply and consistent quality.

2. Best practices

Our Plantations Operations Coordinator and Field Facilitators distribute planting materials and provide plantation establishment and maintenance best practices and facilitate Farm Business School (FBS) programs.

3. Collaboration

We believe that planting in collaboration with smallholder benefit their families and communities, and the environment too. We plant on leased properties or in collaboration with smallholder farmers through contract farming.

4. Access to Capital

We facilitate sustainable and impactful capital flow to farmers through our Rolling Fund, Equity Crowdfunding, and Rewards Crowdfunding.

5. Our Impact

Our bamboo-based agroforestry systems improves soil health, improves economic resilience of the farmers, reduce poverty and improve livelihoods, and address climate change adaptation and mitigation.

Our Impact

Agroforestry is seen as a catalyst for creating shared value among an integrated value chain and a means to create socio-economic synergies and enhance livelihoods alongside with climate change mitigation and adaptation.


In bamboo-based agroforestry systems, the bamboos play important role in the livelihoods of rural people by providing employment, energy, nutritious foods, and a wide range of goods and also balancing ecosystems. Bamboo-based agroforestry systems are currently being promoted as a viable land-use option to reduce dependence on natural forest for wood, fuelwood, and construction materials.
